01 — Problem Statement

The market exists. Privacy doesn't.

Invoice financing is a $3 trillion global market. SMEs across Lagos, Nairobi, Jakarta, and Karachi hold billions in unpaid invoices from Tier-1 corporates and government entities. Lenders have idle capital seeking real-world yield. The mechanism is understood. The demand is proven. Yet the market has not moved on-chain at scale.

The reason is singular and specific: privacy. To finance an invoice on-chain today, an SME must expose their client relationships, invoice amounts, buyer identity, and payment history — permanently and publicly — to every competitor, regulator, and observer on the network. No serious business accepts this. Centrifuge proved the mechanics work. Goldfinch proved the demand is real in emerging markets. Neither solved privacy. Neither scaled.

FHE is the only cryptographic primitive that enables computation on encrypted data without decryption. The credit score, the yield calculation, the compliance check — all run on ciphertext. Nothing touches plaintext on-chain, ever.

Core technical thesis

02 — Product Vision

What Obscura is

Obscura is a privacy-preserving invoice financing protocol. SMEs tokenize unpaid invoices as encrypted real-world assets. Lenders finance them without seeing the underlying data. Compliance proofs are generated on demand. The protocol runs on Fhenix FHE for computation on ciphertext and Privara for compliant payment rails.

Product statement
Obscura turns unpaid invoices into private, financeable on-chain assets — giving SMEs liquidity without exposing their business, and giving lenders yield without requiring trust in plaintext data.
For SMEs
Working capital access
Submit an invoice, receive a stablecoin advance within the same session. No collateral. No exposed client relationships. No competitor intelligence leakage.
For lenders
Real-world yield with privacy
Browse risk scores, fund invoices, earn financing fees. Never see invoice amounts or buyer identities. Risk is assessed by FHE — not by trust.
For regulators
Selective disclosure on demand
Threshold proofs generated without exposing underlying data. Compliance without surveillance.
Primary markets
Africa, SE Asia, MENA
SME credit gaps are largest where privacy requirements are highest and traditional rails are weakest.
03 — Users

Who this is built for

Two primary actors, equal in importance. The protocol only works when both are present — SMEs need lenders to fund invoices, lenders need SMEs to generate yield.

Primary actor 01
The SME Originator
A business in an emerging market holding unpaid invoices from a creditworthy buyer. They need liquidity now — not in 90 days when the invoice settles. They cannot afford to expose their client relationships or business volume on a public ledger.
Submit an invoice and receive a stablecoin advance quickly
Keep buyer identity and invoice amount private
Repay at maturity when the buyer pays them
Build a credit history that improves future terms
Meet compliance requirements without full disclosure
Primary actor 02
The Lender
A DeFi participant, African asset manager, or institutional capital allocator seeking real-world yield. They want verified, risk-scored receivables — but not the legal and reputational exposure of seeing sensitive SME business data.
Browse invoices by risk score without seeing raw data
Fund invoices and earn a financing fee
Trust that credit scoring is computed, not claimed
Trigger escalation if a borrower defaults
Maintain compliance without holding sensitive counterparty data

05 — Wave Breakdown

Five waves. Each one complete.

Every wave is a fully working slice of the protocol — not a partial build or a stub. Each wave has a primary workflow, defined user stories, and acceptance criteria that can be tested from end to end before the next wave begins. Waves stack on each other. Nothing is thrown away.

Wave 01
Corporate Invoice — End to End
Buildathon scope
Fhenix + Privara · Mock data · Deployed contracts
A Lagos FMCG supplier submits an $80,000 invoice from Unilever Nigeria, receives a stablecoin advance, and a lender funds it — all within a single deployed dApp with real Fhenix FHE encryption and Privara payment rails. The full corporate invoice lifecycle runs end to end with mock buyer oracle data.
US-01 Wallet connection & KYB onboarding SME
As an SME, I want to connect my wallet and complete business verification so that I can access the protocol and submit invoices.
  • SME connects wallet via standard web3 modal
  • Privara KYB flow launches and collects business credentials
  • Encrypted credential minted to CredentialRegistry.sol as ciphertext
  • SME receives a verified status on their dashboard — no raw credential data visible on-chain
  • Unverified wallets cannot submit invoices
US-02 Invoice submission & encryption SME
As an SME, I want to submit an invoice so that it is encrypted and tokenized as a private RWA without exposing my buyer or invoice amount to anyone.
  • SME inputs invoice amount, buyer name, and due date in the dashboard
  • Fhenix coprocessor encrypts all invoice fields on submission
  • InvoiceVault.sol mints a private ERC-1155 token representing the invoice
  • Token appears on the financing marketplace — amount and buyer name not visible
  • SME dashboard confirms submission with token ID and encrypted status
US-03 FHE credit scoring Protocol
As the protocol, I want to compute a credit risk score on encrypted invoice data so that lenders can assess risk without seeing any raw invoice information.
  • CreditOracle.sol runs FHE computation on encrypted invoice fields
  • Checks: invoice amount within pool range, buyer creditworthiness above threshold (mock oracle), due date within tenor
  • Returns a risk score (e.g. A–, 87/100) — no raw data exposed
  • Score attached to the invoice token on the marketplace
  • Invoices failing the credit check are flagged as ineligible — SME notified without reason detail
US-04 Lender onboarding & marketplace access Lender
As a lender, I want to connect my wallet and access the financing marketplace so that I can browse invoices by risk score and fund them.
  • Lender connects wallet and completes accredited lender verification via Privara
  • Lender dashboard displays available invoices showing: risk score, advance amount, discount rate, tenor — nothing else
  • Invoice amounts and buyer identities are not shown at any point
  • Lender can filter by risk tier and tenor
US-05 Invoice funding & advance disbursement Lender + SME
As a lender, I want to fund an invoice at a discount rate so that the SME receives a stablecoin advance and I hold a claim on the full settlement amount at maturity.
  • Lender selects an invoice and confirms funding terms (advance %, discount rate)
  • SME accepts terms on-chain
  • FinancingPool.sol locks the invoice token in escrow
  • Privara releases USDC advance to SME wallet
  • Both dashboards update: SME shows advance received, lender shows active position
US-06 Repayment & auto-settlement SME + Lender
As an SME, I want to repay the advance at maturity so that the lender receives their principal plus financing fee and the invoice token is closed.
  • SME triggers repayment from their dashboard
  • FinancingPool.sol auto-settles: lender receives principal + fee
  • Invoice token burned, position closed on both dashboards
  • Transaction confirmed on-chain with settlement receipt
Wave 02
Government Contract & Freelancer Flows
Post-hackathon
Additional pool tiers · Reputation scoring begins
Two new invoice types go live — a Kenyan construction firm financing a $500K government contract receivable, and a Nigerian developer advancing a $15K freelancer invoice from a UK client. The government-backed pool tier activates. Encrypted reputation scoring begins accumulating.
US-07 Government contract verification SME
As an SME with a government contract, I want to submit both the invoice and the signed contract reference so that my invoice is classified as government-backed and scores higher.
  • SME uploads invoice + government contract reference number
  • Fhenix encrypts both documents; contract reference stored as ciphertext
  • FHE verifies contract reference matches a known government entity (encrypted oracle)
  • Token flagged as government-backed tier — elevated risk score automatically applied
  • Buyer identity (the Ministry) remains hidden throughout
US-08 Freelancer KYC & retail pool access Individual
As an individual freelancer, I want to complete a lighter KYC process and access the retail pool so that I can finance my invoices without the business verification required of SMEs.
  • Individual KYC via Privara (lighter than KYB — no business registration required)
  • Encrypted credential: verified individual, jurisdiction, non-sanctioned
  • Freelancer invoices routed to retail pool (ticket size $1K–$50K)
  • FHE checks client is a registered company in their jurisdiction (encrypted registry oracle)
US-09 Reputation score accumulation SME + Individual
As a borrower who has repaid an invoice, I want my on-chain reputation score to update so that future invoices score better and I qualify for higher advance rates.
  • Successful repayment triggers encrypted reputation score update in CreditOracle.sol
  • Score is not visible — only its effect on future credit scoring is felt
  • After 3+ repayments, advance rate eligibility increases (85–90%)
  • Score is portable across pool tiers
US-10 Default escalation Lender
As a lender, I want to trigger an escalation if a borrower has not repaid after the grace period so that I can recover my capital with full documentation.
  • Grace period expires — lender can trigger escalation from dashboard
  • Multi-party threshold decryption unlocks buyer identity and invoice amount
  • Lender receives full documentation for legal recovery
  • This is the only moment any invoice data is decrypted in the protocol
Wave 03
Live RWA Wrappers & Privara Full Integration
Post-hackathon
Live oracles · Real compliance rail · FX off-ramp
Mock data is replaced with live oracle feeds. Privara's full compliance rail activates — including real sanctions screening, cross-border stablecoin disbursement, and NGN/KES/IDR FX off-ramp. The protocol becomes usable with real invoices from real businesses.
US-11 Live buyer credit oracle Protocol
As the protocol, I want to query live encrypted buyer credit data from Chainlink so that FHE credit scoring reflects real buyer creditworthiness rather than mock data.
  • Chainlink oracle provides encrypted buyer credit ratings
  • CreditOracle.sol queries live data during FHE scoring computation
  • Credit scores update dynamically as buyer ratings change
  • Raw credit data is never exposed — only the computed score output
US-12 Cross-border disbursement & FX off-ramp SME + Individual
As an SME or freelancer in an emerging market, I want to receive my advance in local currency so that I can use the funds immediately without managing stablecoin conversion myself.
  • Privara handles USDC → NGN / KES / IDR conversion at disbursement
  • SME receives local currency in their nominated bank account or mobile wallet
  • FX rate locked at time of funding agreement
  • Transaction confirmed in both USDC and local currency equivalent on dashboard
US-13 Selective disclosure proof generation SME + Lender
As an SME or lender, I want to generate a compliance proof on demand so that I can satisfy regulatory requirements without revealing invoice data or counterparty identities.
  • Dashboard provides a "Generate proof" action for any active or closed position
  • SME can generate: "total receivables exceed $X", "all buyers are non-sanctioned"
  • Lender can generate: "portfolio exposure below $Y", "all counterparties verified"
  • Proof verified on-chain — no invoice amounts or buyer names revealed
  • Proof downloadable as a signed document for regulatory submission
Wave 04
Permissionless Pool Factory & Audit Interface
Post-hackathon
Pool factory · Auditor UI · Institutional access
Any institution can deploy their own financing pool with custom parameters — ticket size, advance rate, tenor, credential requirements. A dedicated auditor interface allows regulators to request and verify threshold proofs without accessing the main protocol UI.
US-14 Permissionless pool deployment Lender / Institution
As an institution, I want to deploy my own financing pool with custom parameters so that I can run a private credit facility within the Obscura protocol.
  • Pool factory UI allows any verified institution to deploy a pool
  • Parameters: ticket range, advance rate, discount rate, tenor, accepted credential types
  • Pool uses the same four core contracts — no new code required
  • Pool visible on marketplace immediately after deployment
US-15 Auditor proof verification interface Auditor / Regulator
As a regulator, I want a dedicated interface to request and verify compliance proofs from Obscura participants so that I can perform regulatory oversight without accessing the main protocol UI.
  • Auditor interface separate from SME and lender dashboards
  • Auditor submits a proof request to a participant
  • Participant approves the request and a threshold proof is generated
  • Auditor verifies the proof on-chain — receives a yes/no or aggregate result
  • No invoice data, counterparty names, or business relationships are revealed
Wave 05
Protocol Maturity — Scale & Governance
Long-term
Governance · Secondary market · Protocol token
Obscura becomes a self-governing protocol. Invoice tokens are tradeable on a private secondary market. A protocol governance token allows participants to vote on pool parameters, fee structures, and credential requirements. The protocol expands to cross-border trade finance.
US-16 Private secondary market for invoice tokens Lender
As a lender, I want to sell my position in a funded invoice before maturity so that I can manage liquidity without waiting for settlement.
  • Lender can list an active invoice token on the private secondary market
  • Buyers see risk score and remaining tenor — no underlying invoice data
  • Transfer of token transfers the claim on settlement proceeds
  • Original SME repays to contract regardless of token ownership
US-17 Protocol governance Protocol participants
As a protocol participant, I want to vote on pool parameters and fee structures so that the protocol is governed by its users rather than a central team.
  • Governance token distributed to active SMEs, lenders, and pool operators
  • On-chain voting on: pool parameter changes, fee adjustments, new credential types
  • Proposals require a minimum quorum to pass
  • Executed automatically on-chain — no manual intervention required
